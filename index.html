Baseball

Growing up, I’ve always played baseball. When I was younger, I would play soccer in the fall and baseball in the spring, with summer being reserved for traveling and junior lifeguards. However, as I got older, I joined travel baseball and started spending more and more time between the foul lines. Even though I once thought about quitting baseball to play soccer, baseball has always been the steady, reliable sport in my life and I am grateful for it as it has been influential in my life, providing me with a purpose and teaching me lessons for after I hang up my cleats. Now, I spend most of my free time out of schoolwork playing baseball or strength training, with the goal of walking on to my future college’s team to play. I don’t know where my baseball will take me after that, hopefully to the MLB, but for now I have my sights set on collegiate baseball.

Podcast

In addition to playing baseball, I’m also a podcaster. The summer after my freshman year, I started the Meet the Raven Nation podcast , a space where I interview and get to know the students, staff, and alumni of CCA. Since then, I’ve recorded over 50 episodes, and I’ve gotten closer to the people at my school as a result. It wasn’t easy at first finding interviews or convincing people to talk about themselves, but as the show’s developed, I now get approached about interviews and the name Meet the Raven Nation is now familiar around campus. I want to continue doing this show when I come back from college, but am looking to pass it on to the next generation. If you’re interested in going on the show, let me know, and we’ll record a banger episode.

Spanish

No recuerdo la primera vez que escuché una canción de Bad Bunny, pero desde entonces se ha convertido en uno de mis artistas favoritos. Encontré mi pasión por la música española cuando tomé una clase de español en la escuela secundaria. Era una canción de Sebastián Yatra, y eso desarrolló mi interés en el mundo latino y los ritmos que lleva. Ahora, quiero tomar clases de español en la universidad, donde pueda obtener un nivel de español más alto. Pero, por ahora, estoy enfocándome en manteniendo y creciendo el español que yo sé ahora, a través de la música de varios artistas como Quevedo y Jhayco. Siempre estoy dispuesto a encontrar nuevas oportunidades de aprender, especialmente a través de viajar a paises hispanohablantes.
